Your Vistage Talk,
Stripped to What Matters
April 28, 2025 In person · 3 hours
~15–20 key executives
CFOs, CTOs, COOs
⚑ Your instinct will be to teach. Resist it. Your job today is to shift one belief — and leave them wanting the rest.
The Room
Who's in That Chair

These are not the CEOs. They're one rung below — squeezed from the CEO above and the teams below. Young families. High ambition. No slack in the system. They came this far by pushing through, and pushing through is the only move they know.

Fun & hobbies: gone Real friendships: rare One rung left to climb Used to performing, not resting
The Architecture Decision
Go Deep on ONE Chamber — Not Two, Not Four

Your instinct: cover R and E. The better move: go all-in on R (Relational — friendship).

Three hours is enough to introduce four chambers. It's not enough to change anyone with four chambers. One, done right, will do more than four done quickly.

Covering one deeply leaves three unrevealed. That gap is your leverage. They'll want to know what they missed.

3 Beliefs to Dismantle (Opening)
Name What's Already in the Room
🚫 "Rest means stopping."
This is why they can't make themselves do it. Name it first. "You've never been able to rest — and now you know why."
🚫 "I have two lives: work and personal."
Corporate America profits from this lie. Call it out: "When is the last time you were two people? You're not."
🚫 "This is soft skills — not real leadership."
They're sitting in a business meeting. Give them explicit permission to take this seriously before you go further.
The 3-Hour Flow
Pacing That Actually Works
0–30 min
Belief-Dismantling Opening
Name the 3 misbeliefs. Let it land. Don't rush to solutions. This is where they decide if you "get them."
30–150 min
Deep Dive: R — Relational
10–15 min teaching max, then exercise. Repeat. Friendship framework → in-room inventory → partner pairing (see below).
150–180 min
The Punchline + Ask
Reveal the frame. Name what just happened. Introduce the book and the other 3 chambers. Close with one clear action.
The In-Room Exercise That Sticks
Don't Send Them Home Alone With It

At the end of the Relational segment, pair participants with someone in the room who faces the same challenge. Have them commit to one specific, scheduled thing together.

Not: "I'm going to try to make more time for friends."
Yes: "You and I are playing pickleball the first Saturday of next month."

Eric said this group has strong camaraderie. Use it. Built-in accountability makes you memorable.

The Closing Punchline
The Moment That Books You Again
"Here's what we just did for three hours: we dismantled one belief — that rest means inactivity. That's it. That's all we did. And it changes everything.

This was one chamber. There are three more. When all four work together, that's where sustainable leadership actually lives. If you want to know what the other three look like — I wrote the book."
What You Want Walking Out
Define the Win Before You Walk In

1. Kurt books you for a second group. This is the goal. 1 of 3 required pro bono talks — Kurt has 6 groups. Nail this, and #2 and #3 are easier asks.

2. Two or three people stop you after. They want to keep talking. Have your book and a simple next step ready (coaching consult, newsletter, website).
